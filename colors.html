Frequently Asked Questions

Resolving your wall coatings, color selection, and application queries.

Wall color selection is an important process. First, decide whether you want to use a single wall color throughout or a combination of multiple shades in each room. Consider the size of the room and the type of space (bedroom, kids room, living room) since each has a different psychological requirement. Natural lighting also affects perception; vibrant shades work best in bright rooms, while soft tones suit dim areas. Use our color catalog, request a test swatch, and match it against existing furniture components to prevent clashing.
Latest trends embrace organic nature-inspired tones, warm terracotta accents, calm pastel teals, and cozy off-whites. Homeowners are increasingly choosing contrast accent walls (Deep Navy, Royal Gold) inside minimal light gray or white backdrops to create high-end visual depth and startup-like luxury tech aesthetics.
Vastu suggests utilizing light, refreshing, and calming colors to direct positive energy flow. Light yellows, cozy off-whites, and cream tones are highly recommended for living rooms, light pastel blues or greens for master bedrooms to enhance peace, and warm gold accents in worship prayer zones representing spiritual brilliance.
For dark wooden furniture or accents, contrast them with light neutral backdrops like pristine linen or soft off-whites. If you have light, minimalist Scandinavian furniture, you can introduce high-contrast bold backdrops like Deep Navy or Ocean Teal to establish a powerful, sophisticated visual center of gravity in the room.
High light-reflecting whites (like our Shield Gloss White L178), soft pastels (Air Breeze), and warm creams allow light to bounce widely across walls, visually pushing the boundaries of the room. Combining these with a glossy or soft sheen paint finish multiplies this effect, making small bedrooms feel enormously spacious.
